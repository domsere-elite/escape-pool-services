Common questions

What homeowners ask before signing up.

How long does each weekly visit take?

Most residential pools take 30–45 minutes depending on size and shade. We're not in a rush, but we're not lingering either — the goal is consistent quality on a tight route.

Do you come if it's raining or stormy?

Light rain — yes, water chemistry still needs to happen. For lightning, heavy storms, or unsafe conditions we reschedule within 24–48 hours at no extra charge. You'll always get a heads-up text.

Do I need to be home for the visit?

No. All work happens at the pool gate. We just need access — a code, a key in a lockbox, or an unlocked side gate works fine. After every visit you get a service report with photos.

Are chemicals included in the monthly rate?

Yes. All standard chemicals (chlorine, stabilizer, pH and alkalinity balancers) are included in the monthly rate. Specialty treatments — algaecides, phosphate removers, salt for chlorine generators — are quoted separately so you only pay for what your pool actually needs.

How does the free filter cleaning work?

It's a $125 deep clean of your filter — full disassembly, hose-out, inspection, reassembly — that we do at no charge once you sign up for a weekly plan. We schedule it within your first month of service. No catch, no upsell, just a thank-you for trying us.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes. No contracts, no cancellation fees, no auto-renewal traps. Most of our customers stay because they want to, not because they're locked in.

What if my pool is in rough shape?

If it's green or hasn't been serviced in a while, we'll quote a one-time recovery (the "green to clear" treatment) before the weekly schedule starts. Once it's stable, weekly service kicks in at the standard rate.
